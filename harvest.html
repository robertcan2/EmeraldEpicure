How Olives are Harvested

Harvesting of olives is timed to the required maturity of the olive. If green olives are required they are harvested earlier. Ripe black olives are taken later. The harvesting season can be as early as autumn and as late as winter, depending on the location in the world.

Harvesting can be done by hand or by using rake-like tools to pull the olives from the branches. In some locations, olives are dislodged by striking the branches with long staves.

The latest techniques include motor powered rakes that vibrate to shake the olives from their branches. The powered rake allows the harvester to pull the olives down as well. Other mechanized methods use a series of rotating rods that beat the branches.

The ultimate in machinery used is a device that shakes the entire tree by grasping the trunk and vibrating. The olives are caught in a variety of nets, some are laid flat on the ground and others, conical in shape, are attached to the trunk of the tree. Workers wear a collection basket when hand picking.

The method chosen depends on the final use of the olives. Olives destined for curing and the table are harvested by the gentlest method consistent with cost. They are generally harvested by hand. Those used for oil are harvested with one of the mechanized tools since slight damage can be tolerated. In difficult mountainous terrain using machines is dangerous so hand picking is the choice.
