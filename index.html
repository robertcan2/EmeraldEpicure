The Saga of the Olive

When we search for the origin of the olive, we find it began in Anatolia about 6,000 years ago. Anatolia is the peninsula in southwestern Asia that forms the Asian part of Turkey. It then spread south to countries bordering the Mediterranean Sea. Since it was used as a food, for heating, for lighting, as medicine, and as perfume, it became a symbol of wealth and peace.

The History of the Olive

The history of the olive can be traced through ancient recorded accounts. How to cure olives for food was found in Crete and is dated to approximately 3,500 B.C. The Egyptians knew about the olive and recorded that knowledge around 1,000 B.C. Manifests of exported olives and oil are found in Phoenician records. In colonizing Sicily, the Greeks brought olives and olive oil with them.

Cultivation and Harvest

Olive trees must attain maturity before providing acceptable crops of olives. It takes newly planted trees about 8 years to show their first olives. More prolific crops are not attained for another 7 to 12 years. The trees are most comfortable in the warm and temperate areas of the globe. It likes alkaline soil and is subject to frost damage when temperatures fall below -5 degrees Centigrade.

Olive trees, as a resource, provide a wide array of products, among them food for humans as well as animals, oil, tea, cosmetics, health aids, woodware, and furniture. Olives can be cured using a variety of brines and flavorings. They can be filled with an assortment of ingredients from pimentos to pearl onions. Interesting and flavorful pastes or tapenades are made from the chopped olives. Olive oils are available in different flavors, colors, and consistencies for applications in food preparation and enhancement. Olive leaves are processed for tea and are used as fodder for farm animals. The processed leaves are also used as medical and health supplements. The extract from the leaves and the olive oil itself is believed to aid the human cardiovascular system, and have other soothing effects. The wood is prized for manufacturing durable implements and furniture.
